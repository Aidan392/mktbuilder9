The Problem

Most market data is delayed,
aggregated, or unverified.

Published price indices reflect what was reported, not what was paid. Trade data arrives months late. Supplier-provided specs cannot be independently validated.

The result: procurement decisions made on information that does not reflect actual market conditions. Teams negotiate blind, overpay systematically, and miss the signals that reveal where real opportunities and risks are forming.

"If your price data is 90 days old, you're negotiating blind."
Tridge Eye Market Intelligence is built on transaction-level data — not estimates, not surveys, not self-reported figures. Every number is anchored to a real trade that actually happened.
Lagging price benchmarks
Published indices reflect contracts signed months ago. By the time data is public, market conditions have shifted and your negotiation position is already outdated. Teams accept unfavorable terms because they have no current reference point.
Fragmented supply visibility
Production data, harvest estimates, and supply chain relationships are scattered across reports, trade databases, and private networks — impossible to synthesize at the speed procurement decisions require.
Opaque trade relationships
Knowing a supplier exists tells you nothing about who they sell to, at what volume, at what price, or whether they are actually available for your category. Directory listings show presence, not commercial reality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions from
Procurement Teams

What makes this different from published price indices?

Published indices aggregate reported prices, which often lag reality by 30–90 days and reflect what participants chose to report rather than what was actually paid. Tridge Eye is built on verified transaction records — actual trades with real prices, specifications, origins, and counterparties — so the intelligence reflects current market conditions, not historical averages.

Which product categories does Tridge Eye cover?

Tridge Eye covers 6,500+ agri-food product categories, with particular depth in commodity crops, nuts, fruits, vegetables, processed foods, and their key trade corridors. Coverage includes origin-to-destination pricing for major global trade flows, with specification-level data where transaction volume supports it.

How current is the market intelligence?

Tridge Eye incorporates market signals from active trade participants on a continuous basis. Price data is not based on a single publication cycle — it reflects the most recent transaction activity available for that category and corridor. For high-volume categories and major corridors, this means significantly fresher data than published alternatives.

Does this work for categories with fragmented or low-volume trade?

Yes, with appropriate context. For high-volume commodity categories, transaction-level precision is high. For more fragmented or specialized categories, Tridge Eye combines available transaction data with market structure intelligence — supply network mapping, trade relationship patterns, and active participant signals — to provide the most complete picture available.

How does Market Intelligence connect to the rest of Tridge Eye?

Market Intelligence is the research foundation. Once you understand the market structure and price environment, Tridge Eye flows that context into Supplier Discovery — finding which suppliers match your intelligence signals — and Price Benchmarking — validating specific quotes against verified transaction references. The system is designed so each layer adds precision to the next step.

Can this support our internal procurement team rather than replace it?

Yes. Tridge Eye is designed to accelerate and strengthen internal procurement teams — not replace buyer judgment. Your team retains decision authority while the system provides verified intelligence, faster research, and connected workflows that eliminate the manual aggregation and interpretation bottleneck.

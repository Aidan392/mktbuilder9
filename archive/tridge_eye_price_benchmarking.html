The Problem

Most procurement teams negotiate
without knowing what the market pays.

Published price indices arrive months late and reflect what participants chose to report, not what deals were actually closed at. Supplier quotes are presented as market-reflective when they may carry significant margin above what comparable buyers are paying in the same corridor for the same specification.

The result: procurement teams accept prices that feel reasonable because they have no credible alternative reference point. Overpayment is systematic and invisible — because there is no benchmark that reflects current market reality at the level of specificity procurement decisions require.

"The most expensive negotiation is the one where you don't know what fair looks like."
Tridge Eye Price Benchmarking gives you a verified transaction price reference at specification, origin, and corridor level — so you enter every negotiation knowing exactly where you stand relative to what the market is actually paying.
Published indices lag reality by 30–90 days
Price indices are published after data is collected, verified, and processed. By the time a benchmark is publicly available, the market has moved. You are negotiating today with data that reflects what happened last quarter — and suppliers know it.
Supplier quotes are not market evidence
A supplier's quote is not a benchmark. It is their opening position in a negotiation. Without an independent reference point built on actual transaction data, procurement teams have no way to assess whether a quote is market-consistent or margin-loaded.
Regional and specification price spread is invisible
The same product at the same specification can transact at meaningfully different prices depending on origin, destination, and corridor. Without corridor-level transaction data, procurement teams cannot see these spreads — and cannot leverage them in supplier selection or negotiation.

Four High-Stakes Scenarios Where
Benchmarking Changes the Outcome.

Price benchmarking delivers the greatest value in procurement moments where the cost of getting it wrong is real, measurable, and difficult to reverse after the contract is signed.

Scenario 01
A supplier sends you a quote 15% above your expectation
Is this the market, or is this the supplier testing your reference? With verified transaction data at specification level, you know immediately whether the quote reflects market conditions or carries excess margin — and can push back with evidence, not instinct.
Scenario 02
You are evaluating a new origin for the first time
Before committing resources to new supplier development, validate whether prices from that origin are genuinely competitive versus the alternatives you already work with — at your specification and delivery terms, not a commodity average that may not apply to your product.
Scenario 03
Your incumbent supplier raises prices citing market conditions
Verify whether the claimed market movement is real and whether your price increase is proportional to what other buyers in the same market are actually paying. This is the moment when transaction-level benchmarking delivers the clearest, most actionable value.
Scenario 04
You are comparing three shortlisted suppliers before contract
Quotes from three suppliers look similar but you are not certain which represent genuine market pricing. Transaction benchmarks reveal which quotes are market-consistent, which carry room for negotiation, and which may reflect unusual circumstances that need investigation before you commit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions from
Procurement Teams

How is this different from published price indices like CBOT or LME-referenced benchmarks?

Published indices aggregate reported data from market participants and are published on a fixed cycle. They reflect what happened during the reporting period at an aggregated level — often at broad commodity level, not specification level. Tridge Eye is built on verified transaction data — actual closed trades at specific specifications, origins, and corridors — which is both more current and more relevant to specific procurement decisions than a published index.

How specific is the benchmark? Does it account for grade and certification?

Yes. Tridge Eye benchmarks are available at specification level — including grade, certification status, packaging format, and delivery term — for categories where transaction volume supports that level of specificity. This means your reference reflects what buyers with your exact requirements are actually paying, not a commodity average that averages across specifications very different from yours.

How current is the transaction data?

Transaction data is incorporated on a continuous basis as new trade activity is confirmed and structured. For high-volume categories and major corridors, the benchmark reflects very recent market activity. For more specialized categories, the reference period may be slightly longer — but will always be indicated clearly so you can assess its applicability to your current decision.

What if no verified transactions exist for my exact specification?

For categories or specifications with limited transaction volume, Tridge Eye provides the most relevant available benchmark — typically at a slightly broader specification level — along with clear indication of the coverage basis. Supply market context, origin-level pricing signals, and comparable specification data are provided to ensure the output still supports your procurement decision, even without exact specification-level transaction volume.

Can I use this as evidence in a supplier negotiation?

The benchmarks are structured to support that use — presenting your position relative to market with transaction evidence and overpayment signals that can be shared or referenced in a supplier conversation. How you choose to use market data in a specific negotiation is your decision, but the output is designed to be negotiation-ready rather than requiring additional preparation before it can be used.

How does Price Benchmarking connect to the rest of Tridge Eye?

Price Benchmarking often leads directly to two connected actions. If overpayment risk is identified, it can trigger Supplier Discovery — identifying verified alternatives in lower-cost origins — and AI-led outreach to those alternatives. If a target price range is confirmed as market-realistic, it provides context for negotiation preparation and qualification criteria for the outreach campaign.

The Problem

Cocoa procurement teams negotiate
without verified transaction price context.

ICCO reference prices and published indices reflect market sentiment at commodity level — not the specification-level transaction prices that determine whether your incoming quote is market-consistent or margin-loaded. Ivory Coast and Ghana Grade I prices diverge significantly, and certification premiums vary widely by buyer and season.

"Ghana Grade I often trades at a premium to Ivory Coast — but how much is market, and how much is your supplier's margin? Without transaction data, you can't tell."
Tridge Eye Price Benchmarking gives you specification-level transaction price references for cocoa — so you know exactly where your quote stands before you enter the room.
ICCO and published indices lag reality
Published cocoa price benchmarks aggregate reported data at commodity level. They do not reflect current transaction prices at specification level — and suppliers use that gap to maintain margin on buyers without independent benchmarks.
Origin premium opacity
The Ivory Coast vs Ghana price differential shifts with export policy, harvest quality, and seasonal demand. Without current transaction data, procurement teams cannot assess whether the origin premium in their quote is market-reflective or inflated.
Certification premium is unverifiable without data
UTZ, Rainforest Alliance, and Fairtrade premiums vary by buyer, season, and market. Procurement teams paying certification premiums without verified benchmark data often overpay by amounts that compound significantly across annual cocoa volumes.

FAQ

Common Questions from
Cocoa Procurement Teams

How is this different from ICCO prices?

ICCO references aggregate reported prices at commodity level with a publication lag. Tridge Eye is built on verified transaction records — actual closed trades at specific grades, origins, and corridors — providing both greater specificity and greater currency than any published benchmark.

Does this cover both Grade I and fine flavor cocoa?

Yes. Tridge Eye covers mainstream grades (IC and Ghana Grade I and II) and fine flavor categories (Ecuador, Dominican Republic, Peru). Benchmark depth varies — highest for the dominant West Africa corridors, with transaction data supplemented by market intelligence for fine flavor origins.

How current is the cocoa transaction data?

Transaction data is incorporated continuously. For major West Africa corridors and mainstream grades, the benchmark reflects very recent market activity. Seasonal signals — main crop pricing dynamics particularly — are prioritized for currency.

Can this benchmark certification premiums specifically?

Yes. Certification premium benchmarks are available where transaction volume supports specification-level data. For UTZ and Rainforest Alliance in the major West Africa corridors, premium benchmarks are well-supported by transaction evidence.

How does Price Benchmarking connect to Supplier Discovery?

Where benchmarking reveals pricing advantages in alternative origins or grades, the output connects directly to Supplier Discovery — identifying which verified cocoa exporters have transaction history in those parameters and are available for outreach.

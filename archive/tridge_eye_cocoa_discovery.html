The Problem

Cocoa supplier directories reflect
who registered — not who is trading.

The cocoa supply chain is dominated by major trading houses, but the most commercially competitive exporters and cooperatives — especially in Ivory Coast and Ghana — are often mid-tier operators not visible in standard directories. Finding the right cocoa supplier requires knowing who is actually shipping, at what grade, to which buyers.

"The most competitive cocoa exporters in West Africa are not always the ones who show up in international directories."
Tridge Eye Supplier Discovery is built on verified cocoa transaction data — so your shortlist reflects who is actually exporting your specification, not just who has filed a profile.
Trading house concentration
Over-reliance on the Cargill/Barry Callebaut/Olam tier creates pricing leverage risk. Verified mid-tier exporters and cooperative groups often offer more competitive terms — but require transaction intelligence to identify reliably.
Certification and traceability opacity
UTZ, Rainforest Alliance, and Fairtrade supply chains are geographically concentrated and require relationship-level intelligence to navigate. Self-reported certification claims cannot be independently verified without transaction context.
Seasonal sourcing windows are narrow
Main crop and mid-crop windows create time pressure on supplier qualification. Knowing which exporters have available certified supply before the window opens requires advance intelligence — not reactive directory search.

FAQ

Common Questions from
Cocoa Procurement Teams

How does this differ from cocoa trader registrations?

Trader registrations reflect who has filed a profile. Tridge Eye reflects who has actually shipped cocoa — at what grade, in what volume, to which buyers, through which channels — so every supplier on your shortlist has demonstrated commercial activity, not just registered intent.

Does this cover cooperatives as well as large exporters?

Yes. Tridge Eye includes cooperative groups and mid-tier exporters alongside the major trading houses — particularly in Ivory Coast and Ghana, where cooperative supply often offers the most competitive pricing for certified grades.

How current is the supplier intelligence?

Supplier intelligence is updated continuously as new transaction data is confirmed. Seasonal availability signals — particularly relevant for main crop and mid-crop timing — are prioritized for currency.

Can this find non-GMO or specialty cocoa suppliers?

Yes. Fine flavor, organic, and specialty grade cocoa suppliers are covered, with intelligence depth varying by origin and certification tier. Ecuador and Dominican Republic specialty networks are particularly well-covered.

How does Supplier Discovery connect to the rest of Tridge Eye?

The shortlist flows directly into AI-led outreach and into Price Benchmarking — so identified suppliers can be contacted and their quotes validated against verified transaction price references in one connected workflow.

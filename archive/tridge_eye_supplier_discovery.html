The Problem

A directory tells you who exists.
Tridge Eye tells you who is relevant.

Most supplier databases are built from self-reported registrations. A supplier lists themselves. You find them. You have no way to know whether they are actually trading in your category, at your volume, at your specification — or whether they are a legitimate exporter or a broker who will waste three weeks of your team's time.

The result: procurement teams spend enormous effort on initial screening that yields few qualified options. The suppliers who look right on paper often aren't, and the ones who are right for your category often aren't discoverable through traditional channels.

"Most procurement teams don't have a supplier problem. They have a supplier intelligence problem."
Tridge Eye Supplier Discovery replaces directory-based search with transaction-verified intelligence — so every supplier on your shortlist has earned their position through demonstrated commercial activity, not just a self-filed profile.
Self-reported profiles with no verification
Supplier directories are filled by the suppliers themselves. There is no verification of what they actually export, at what scale, to which buyers, or at what quality level. The information you get reflects what they want you to think, not what the market knows about them.
No intent or availability signals
A database record tells you a supplier exists. It tells you nothing about whether they are actively selling right now, whether they have capacity available, or whether they are in the process of switching buyers and looking for new relationships in your category.
Hidden suppliers in fragmented markets
The most commercially relevant suppliers in many agri-food categories are not discoverable through traditional directories. They don't list themselves publicly. They operate through networks. Tridge Eye surfaces them through transaction intelligence — not registration data.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions from
Procurement Teams

How is this different from a supplier database?

A supplier database is built from registrations — suppliers self-filing their profile. Tridge Eye Supplier Discovery is built from verified transaction data — what suppliers have actually shipped, to which buyers, at which specifications, at what scale, through which channels. The difference is not a matter of depth. It is a different data source that produces fundamentally more reliable results.

How current is the supplier intelligence?

Supplier intelligence in Tridge Eye reflects the most recent transaction activity available for that category and origin. The network is continuously updated through new transaction data, so supplier profiles reflect current market participation — not a registration that may be years old. Intent and switching signals are particularly time-sensitive and are prioritized accordingly.

Can this find suppliers in markets that are hard to access through normal channels?

Yes. Many of the most commercially relevant suppliers in agri-food categories do not register on directories or list themselves publicly. Tridge Eye surfaces them through transaction intelligence — if they are trading in your category and corridor, they appear in the network regardless of whether they have self-registered anywhere.

Does this work for specialty or low-volume categories?

Yes, with appropriate scoping. For high-volume commodity categories, transaction intelligence is dense and scoring is highly precise. For specialty categories, the intelligence combines available transaction data with supply network mapping, certification records, and market participation signals to produce the most complete supplier picture available.

How does Supplier Discovery connect to outreach?

The shortlist produced by Supplier Discovery flows directly into AI-led outreach. Each shortlisted supplier can be contacted through structured, specification-aware outreach campaigns — responses are captured, classified, and tracked. Discovery and outreach are not separate tools requiring manual handoff between them.

How many suppliers are in the Tridge network?

The Tridge network includes 350,000+ registered companies across 140+ countries, with active transaction context for the agri-food categories covered by Tridge Eye. The supplier discovery output is not limited to registered participants — it also surfaces suppliers identified through transaction intelligence who have not self-registered in the network.
